## Decisions

### APPROVED — Add dashboard-case handoff from Ruegefrist results

**User-impact note:** Target users are Swiss construction owners, contractors, and project admins who finish a public Ruegefrist calculation and need to track that deadline as an operational compliance case. The workflow moment is immediately after calculation, before the user manually re-enters the same legally sensitive dates elsewhere. This is not just incremental polish: it turns a one-off legal calculator result into a prefilled case workflow, reducing transcription risk and helping users move from legal insight to audit-ready tracking.

**Engineering task:** In `components/ruegefrist-calculator.tsx`, add a result CTA to `/dashboard/cases?contract=<calculated contract date>&discovery=<calculated discovery date>`. In `app/dashboard/cases/page.tsx`, sanitize and consume valid `contract`/`discovery` query params, open the create-case form with those dates prefilled, and remove consumed/invalid handoff params while preserving valid filters/search/unrelated params. Add focused tests in `__tests__/ruegefrist-calculator.test.tsx` and `__tests__/cases-url-sync.test.tsx`.

### BACKLOG — First activity-timeline derivation helper for compliance cases

Strategically strong, but this pure-model phase is not user-visible today and would be a larger review surface than the handoff slice. Keep as a future medium candidate when the pipeline can spend a full run on timeline fixtures and contract naming.

### BACKLOG — Guided public-to-dashboard onboarding flow after legal calculation

High-value direction, but too broad for one autonomous daily PR because it crosses auth, onboarding, cases, vault, reminders, and conversion copy. The approved handoff is the right phase-1 slice.
