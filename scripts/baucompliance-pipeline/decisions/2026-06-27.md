## Decisions

### APPROVED — Associate Ruegefrist calculator visible date labels with their date inputs

**User-impact note:** Target users are Swiss construction owners, contractors, and project admins entering legally sensitive contract/discovery dates in the public Ruegefrist calculator. The workflow moment is starting a date calculation that may become an ICS reminder or shared legal deadline link. This is not just incremental polish: the calculator is a trust/conversion surface, and visible legal labels should directly control the exact inputs whose values drive deadline and reminder exports.

**Engineering task:** In `components/ruegefrist-calculator.tsx`, add stable IDs to the contract and discovery date inputs and connect the visible labels with `htmlFor`. Add a focused regression test in `__tests__/ruegefrist-calculator.test.tsx` proving each visible label is associated with the matching input.

### BACKLOG — Public-to-dashboard case handoff from calculated Ruegefrist result

Strong Medium product bridge from a public legal calculation into tracked compliance workflow, but it should not be squeezed into the same run without first confirming an existing dashboard destination can safely consume the prefilled date contract. Future phase-1: add a tested destination query contract or a non-invasive CTA if the destination already exists.

### BACKLOG — Case activity timeline across protocols, vault evidence, checklist, reminders, and finalization

Strategically compelling, but too large for one daily autonomous PR. The next credible phase-1 remains a pure timeline derivation helper with fixtures before UI or persistence.
