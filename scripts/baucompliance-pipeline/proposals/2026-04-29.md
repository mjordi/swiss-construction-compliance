# Research Proposals — 2026-04-29

## PM Insights (market + repo signal)
1. Compliance products in construction are winning on "defensible records" UX, so explicit confirmation states (not just free text) improve trust and legal readiness.
2. Review velocity in this repo still favors tightly scoped wizard hardening over broad new modules; quality-of-input upgrades are the safest compounding path.
3. Multi-lingual consistency remains a differentiator in Swiss workflows, so data-integrity changes should always ship with all supported locales.

## Proposal S — Signature panel helper when finalize is blocked
- **Size**: Small
- **Problem**: Step 2 can feel opaque when completion conditions are not explicit.
- **User value**: Reduces retry loops and support confusion.
- **Implementation sketch**: Add inline helper copy under signature section describing remaining completion requirement.
- **Risk**: Low

## Proposal M — Defect capture guard with explicit "no visible defects" confirmation
- **Size**: Medium
- **Problem**: Protocols can be finalized with an empty defect section and no explicit statement, weakening record quality.
- **User value**: Improves legal defensibility and completeness of every finalized protocol.
- **Implementation sketch**: Require either defect text or a checkbox confirming no visible defects; keep signature requirement intact; localize new copy in de/fr/it/en.
- **Risk**: Medium-Low

## Proposal L — Supabase-backed protocol draft sync across devices
- **Size**: Large
- **Problem**: Draft continuity breaks when field and office users switch devices.
- **User value**: Strong reduction in rework and missed documentation during handovers.
- **Implementation sketch**: Introduce draft persistence table, optimistic autosave, conflict policy, and retention controls.
- **Risk**: High
