# Daily Product Improvement Proposals — 2026-07-05

## PM insights

Primary user lens: Swiss construction owners, contractors, and project administrators who move between defect cases, deadline reminders, protocols, and the evidence vault to prove that required notice/evidence steps were completed.

Workflow moment: after a case has been created and evidence/protocol records exist, the user scans a dashboard surface to decide whether the project is audit-ready, needs notice drafting, needs evidence, or can be safely archived.

Best-in-market expectation: a Swiss construction-compliance product should make legal readiness obvious at the moment of triage, not hide it behind a bare percentage or require opening each case. It should also preserve confidence that exported reminders, case next actions, and evidence readiness all describe the same workflow state.

Recent pipeline pattern: the last 14 days were mostly user-facing S-to-bounded-M improvements around URL-state reliability, public-to-dashboard handoffs, case next-action visibility, deadline reminder accessibility, and reminder export summaries. A safe adjacent slice should strengthen the vault/case bridge without expanding into a new activity timeline workspace.

## Proposal S — Label vault compliance readiness with completed checklist counts

- **Target user and job-to-be-done:** Project admins and contractors scanning the Technical Vault to decide which projects are audit-ready before archiving or opening cases.
- **Problem:** Vault cards show a compliance percentage, but not the concrete completed/total checklist count behind it. Users can see “50%” without knowing whether that means one missing legal step or several missing audit artifacts.
- **Scope:** In `app/dashboard/vault/page.tsx`, carry the existing `deriveChecklistProgress` result into each vault card and render a localized compact readiness label next to the compliance percentage, for example `2/4 checklist items ready`. Add focused tests proving the count appears for persisted/default checklist state.
- **Expected impact:** Makes existing vault triage more trustworthy without changing persistence, routing, or legal calculations.
- **User-visible outcome:** Each project card tells users both the percentage and the exact checklist progress count before they open the case.
- **Competitive / best-product rationale:** Audit-ready construction software should explain readiness signals, not display opaque scores.
- **Risk (1-10):** 2
- **Effort/size:** S
- **Phase-1 slice:** N/A

## Proposal M — Add a vault evidence-readiness summary by missing legal step

- **Target user and job-to-be-done:** Owners/admins reviewing multiple projects who need to know whether evidence, notice drafting, or calendar reminders are blocking legal readiness.
- **Problem:** The vault currently compresses readiness to status, docs count, and percentage. Users cannot tell which missing compliance step requires action without opening cases.
- **Scope:** Extend vault card view models with a compact missing-step summary derived from checklist progress/defaults, localize labels, and add tests for missing evidence/notice/reminder states.
- **Expected impact:** Faster triage and fewer missed legal prerequisites across evidence and notice workflows.
- **User-visible outcome:** Cards would say which step is next, such as “Attach evidence next” or “Draft notice next,” not only “50%”.
- **Competitive / best-product rationale:** Best-in-market tools surface the next bottleneck directly in the workspace where the user is triaging.
- **Risk (1-10):** 4
- **Effort/size:** M
- **Phase-1 slice:** Start with read-only missing-step text on vault cards, deferring new filters/actions.

## Proposal L — Unified compliance activity cockpit across cases, vault, protocols, and reminders

- **Target user and job-to-be-done:** Construction teams managing many defect workflows who need one command center for legal deadlines, evidence completeness, protocol finalization, and reminder exports.
- **Problem:** Recent improvements connect individual surfaces, but users still assemble readiness across separate pages.
- **Scope:** Create a unified read-only activity cockpit that combines case timelines, protocol counts, vault evidence state, reminders, and next legal actions, then phase toward guided task completion.
- **Expected impact:** Step-change toward the best Swiss construction-compliance product by replacing scattered surfaces with an audit-ready operational view.
- **User-visible outcome:** A single dashboard answers “what needs my attention today and why legally?”
- **Competitive / best-product rationale:** Market-leading compliance products turn legal obligations into a prioritized action queue with traceable evidence.
- **Risk (1-10):** 8
- **Effort/size:** L / strategic
- **Phase-1 slice:** A read-only “next bottleneck” helper or vault-card missing-step summary after the simpler count label proves the contract.
