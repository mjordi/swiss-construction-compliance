# Research Proposals — 2026-04-12

## PM Insights (from market + repo signal)
1. Contractors working across multiple active projects need lower-friction triage loops; reducing repeated filter setup in Cases has immediate daily value.
2. Recent repository delivery remains strongest for tightly scoped UX improvements that do not require schema changes or background jobs.
3. Strategic product leverage is still in operational reliability (deadlines, follow-through, searchable case state), not in broad new surface area.

## Proposal S — Persistent “Clear filters” action in Cases filter bar
- **Size**: Small
- **Problem**: Users can reset filters only via individual controls (or a no-results fallback), which adds extra clicks during repeated triage passes.
- **User value**: Faster return to default view when status/regime/search filters are active.
- **Implementation sketch**: Show a compact “Clear filters” button directly in the filter panel whenever any filter is active.
- **Risk**: Low

## Proposal M — Quick filter presets (local, per user)
- **Size**: Medium
- **Problem**: Teams frequently re-apply the same regime/status/sort combinations.
- **User value**: One-click entry into common triage views.
- **Implementation sketch**: Add save/apply/delete preset controls backed by local persisted state and URL-sync compatibility.
- **Risk**: Medium

## Proposal L — Shared deadline digest with escalation routing
- **Size**: Large
- **Problem**: Deadline oversight depends too heavily on manual checks by individual users.
- **User value**: Team-level accountability and lower risk of missed notice windows.
- **Implementation sketch**: Scheduled digest generation, assignee escalation states, and activity log.
- **Risk**: High
