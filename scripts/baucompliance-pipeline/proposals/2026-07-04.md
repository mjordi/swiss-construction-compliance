# Daily Product Improvement Proposals — 2026-07-04

## PM insights

Primary users are Swiss construction owners, contractors, and project admins who calculate legally sensitive defect/warranty deadlines and then need to share or export reminders without losing confidence in what will be scheduled. The workflow moment is after a deadline calculation, just before copying a link or downloading an `.ics` file. A best-in-market Swiss construction-compliance product should make reminder artifacts audit-clear: users should not need to infer which reminder offsets will be embedded in a shared link or calendar export.

Recent decisions over the last 14 days show mostly user-facing reliability, accessibility, URL-state trust, and cross-workflow handoff improvements. Cases has two active open PRs (#148 and #149), so cases/timeline work has overlap risk today. The latest merged PR (#150) named reminder preset groups on deadline calculators, making a reminder-export summary the natural next phase without changing deadline math or cases workflows.

## S — Show selected reminder offsets next to deadline export/share actions

- **Target user and job-to-be-done:** Project admins exporting or sharing legal deadline reminders who need to verify which reminders will be included.
- **Problem:** After selecting reminder presets, the result actions show share/download controls but no compact confirmation of the selected offsets at the action moment.
- **Scope:** On the public Ruegefrist calculator and dashboard deadlines page, render a concise reminder summary beside the share/download action area once results exist.
- **Expected impact:** Reduces export/share uncertainty and makes reminder artifacts more auditable.
- **User-visible outcome:** Users see `Calendar reminders: 14 days, 7 days, 1 days` (or the selected/none state) near the actions that use those presets.
- **Competitive / best-product rationale:** Deadline tools should make legal reminder artifacts reviewable before export, not only configurable upstream.
- **Risk (1-10):** 2.
- **Effort/size:** S.
- **Phase-1 slice:** Implement text summary only; no copy redesign or new reminder scheduling workflow.

## M — Add a deadline reminder review card before `.ics` download

- **Target user and job-to-be-done:** Team leads preparing calendar exports for multiple stakeholders who want a richer pre-export review.
- **Problem:** The app generates ICS artifacts directly; users cannot review all dates/reminders as a single export package.
- **Scope:** Add a compact review card listing selected reminders and export target deadlines, on both deadline surfaces.
- **Expected impact:** Improves confidence before distributing deadline artifacts.
- **User-visible outcome:** A structured pre-export summary of dates and reminder offsets.
- **Competitive / best-product rationale:** Best legal workflow tools show a human-readable audit preview before generating external artifacts.
- **Risk (1-10):** 4.
- **Effort/size:** M.
- **Phase-1 slice:** Limit to read-only review card; no new ICS format changes.

## L — Unified deadline-to-action cockpit across calculators, cases, and protocol finalization

- **Target user and job-to-be-done:** Project teams managing defect deadlines across public calculators, cases, evidence, and protocols.
- **Problem:** Deadline insights, reminder exports, case action readiness, and evidence/protocol status are spread across surfaces.
- **Scope:** A unified read-only dashboard model/card that aggregates deadlines, next legal action, reminders, evidence readiness, and protocol finalization status.
- **Expected impact:** High strategic value: users can prioritize legal actions from one cockpit.
- **User-visible outcome:** A central action panel showing what deadline is next and what evidence/action is missing.
- **Competitive / best-product rationale:** Market-leading construction compliance software should turn legal timelines into operational next actions.
- **Risk (1-10):** 8.
- **Effort/size:** L.
- **Phase-1 slice:** Start with a pure timeline/action derivation helper once active cases PRs settle.
