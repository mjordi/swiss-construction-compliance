# Daily Product Improvement Proposals — 2026-07-24

Workflow moment: while reviewing a case before sending a notice, finalizing a protocol, or preparing an audit file, the user currently sees dates and readiness signals in separate cells but not as one chronological legal sequence.

Best-in-market expectation: a construction-compliance product should turn contract, defect discovery, statutory notice deadline, evidence, and protocol state into an immediately understandable, audit-ready chronology rather than making users mentally order disconnected facts.

## Proposal S — Show case creation and last-update timestamps in details

- **Target user and job-to-be-done:** Project administrator checking whether a case record was recently maintained.
- **Problem:** The Cases ledger does not expose database creation/update timestamps.
- **Scope:** Add localized created/updated metadata to case details.
- **Expected impact:** Improves basic record recency awareness.
- **User-visible outcome:** Two timestamps appear in expanded case details.
- **Competitive / best-product rationale:** Audit tools should expose record metadata, but timestamps alone do not explain the legal sequence.
- **Risk:** 2/10.
- **Effort/size:** S.
- **Phase-1 slice:** N/A.

## Proposal M — Add a first legal milestone timeline to case details

- **Target user and job-to-be-done:** Contractor, owner, or project administrator reviewing a defect case before notice, protocol, or audit action.
- **Problem:** Contract date, defect discovery, notice deadline, and current next action are displayed as disconnected facts, forcing users to reconstruct chronology mentally.
- **Scope:** Add a pure helper that derives an ordered read-only milestone model from the existing case view model; render contract, discovery, and applicable notice-deadline milestones inside expanded case details; localize labels and add helper/UI regression coverage. Do not change deadline math, persistence, filters, exports, or routes.
- **Expected impact:** Makes the legal sequence scannable and establishes a safe phase-1 contract for the recurring audit/activity cockpit.
- **User-visible outcome:** Expanded case details show an ordered legal milestone trail with dates and a clear pending deadline milestone where applicable.
- **Competitive / best-product rationale:** Best-in-market compliance software should make the legally relevant sequence self-explanatory and audit-ready, not merely show a collection of status cards.
- **Risk:** 3/10.
- **Effort/size:** M, bounded to a pure derivation helper, one existing page, translations, and focused tests.
- **Phase-1 slice:** Static legal milestones from already-derived case dates; protocol/evidence events with their own timestamps remain future phases.

## Proposal L — Build a persisted cross-workflow audit activity ledger

- **Target user and job-to-be-done:** Compliance lead assembling a defensible record across Cases, Protocols, Vault evidence, reminders, and exports.
- **Problem:** Artifact actions and lifecycle changes are distributed and do not form a durable chronological audit trail.
- **Scope:** Event schema, persistence/migrations, writers for case/protocol/evidence/reminder actions, timeline UI, filters, and export.
- **Expected impact:** Creates a major strategic audit-defensibility capability.
- **User-visible outcome:** One authoritative activity ledger per defect dossier.
- **Competitive / best-product rationale:** A durable, exportable proof chain is a market-defining capability for legal construction compliance.
- **Risk:** 8/10.
- **Effort/size:** L.
- **Phase-1 slice:** First validate the milestone presentation and ordering contract with the Medium proposal before introducing event persistence.
